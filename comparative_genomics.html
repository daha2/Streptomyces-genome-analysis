Comparative Genomics of Streptomyces silvae

This page summarizes the comparative genomic analysis of strains within the S. silvae clade, including Average Nucleotide Identity (ANI), genome-wide synteny analysis using progressiveMauve, and comparison of biosynthetic gene clusters (BGCs) based on antiSMASH.

Together, these analyses provide insight into the genomic coherence, structural diversification, and biosynthetic potential of the investigated strains.

Key findings. All analyzed genomes belong to the same species-level cluster, with ANI values ranging from 96.65% to 100%. Despite this high overall genomic similarity, the clade shows clear intraspecific diversification. Genome-wide alignment reveals largely conserved synteny together with strain-specific rearrangements, most prominently in Streptomyces sp. gb1(2016). BGC profiling shows a conserved core biosynthetic repertoire alongside strain-specific and potentially novel clusters.

Dataset

  • S. silvae For3T – type strain
  • Streptomyces sp. M3
  • S. silvae TMS4I1
  • S. silvae TMS12I2
  • Streptomyces sp. gb1(2016)
  • Streptomyces sp. ADI93-02
  • Streptomyces sp. LamerLS–316
  • Streptomyces sp. SID4921

Average Nucleotide Identity (ANI)

Pairwise ANI analysis confirmed the assignment of all investigated strains to the species S. silvae. Even the lowest ANI value within the dataset (96.65%) remains clearly above the widely accepted 95% species threshold. This indicates a highly coherent species cluster with pronounced internal diversification.

Table 1. Pairwise ANI values (%) calculated for the analyzed S. silvae strains. Values above 95% indicate assignment to the same species. The highest sequence identity was observed between Streptomyces sp. LamerLS–316 and Streptomyces sp. SID4921 (100%), followed by S. silvae For3T and Streptomyces sp. M3 (99.45%).
Main observation

ANI values support a taxonomically stable species group with high overall sequence conservation.

Biological implication

The genomes are closely related but not identical, indicating meaningful intraspecific diversification rather than species-level separation.

Genome Synteny and Structural Variation

To investigate genome architecture across the S. silvae clade, a whole-genome multiple alignment was performed using progressiveMauve. The alignment reveals extensive synteny across the analyzed genomes, together with local rearrangements, inversions, and strain-specific structural variation.

Figure 1. MAUVE alignment of S. silvae isolates. Multiple genome alignment was performed using progressiveMauve with S. silvae For3T as reference genome. Colored local collinear blocks (LCBs) represent homologous genomic regions; inverted segments are shown below the midline. Shifts and inversions reflect structural genomic variation among strains.

Most conserved pair

Streptomyces sp. LamerLS–316 and SID4921 show complete synteny, consistent with their 100% ANI identity.

Most rearranged genome

Streptomyces sp. gb1(2016) exhibits the strongest rearrangements, fragmentations, and inversions within the clade.

Interpretation. Genome organization is largely conserved across the clade, indicating strong relatedness at the level of overall chromosome structure. At the same time, strain-specific rearrangements suggest microevolutionary differentiation, especially in gb1(2016), while other strains remain highly stable in genome architecture.

Biosynthetic Gene Clusters (BGCs)

Biosynthetic gene clusters were identified using antiSMASH 7.0 in order to compare the secondary metabolic potential across the S. silvae clade. The results reveal both a highly conserved core set of BGCs and several strain-specific or weakly characterized clusters, indicating a combination of metabolic stability and diversification.

Table 2. Biosynthetic gene clusters (BGCs) identified in the analyzed S. silvae genomes and their similarity (%) to known reference clusters according to antiSMASH 7.0. Higher similarity values indicate close relatedness to characterized clusters, while lower values suggest divergent or potentially novel biosynthetic pathways.
Conserved core BGCs

Core clusters conserved across all strains include isorenieratene, melanin, alkylresorcinol, ectoine, and desferrioxamine B/E, all with 100% similarity to known reference clusters.

Strain-specific diversity

The genomes of Streptomyces sp. gb1(2016) and ADI93-02 contain the highest number of unique or uncommon clusters, pointing to increased biosynthetic diversification.

Interpretation. The BGC profiles indicate that S. silvae combines a conserved species-level metabolic backbone with additional strain-specific biosynthetic traits. Several clusters annotated with very low similarity values (e.g. tetronasin, lactonamycin, hexacosalactone A) may represent previously uncharacterized or strongly diverged pathways.

Methods

  • Read processing and genome assembly: Galaxy workflow
  • Reference-guided scaffolding: RagTag
  • Reference genome for scaffolding: Streptomyces sp. M3
  • Genome annotation: RASTtk
  • ANI calculation: FastANI
  • Whole-genome alignment: progressiveMauve
  • BGC prediction: antiSMASH 7.0

Summary

Comparative genomics of the S. silvae clade reveals a taxonomically coherent yet evolutionarily dynamic species group. High ANI values support species-level unity, while genome-wide alignment and BGC profiling uncover structural plasticity and diversified biosynthetic potential among strains.
