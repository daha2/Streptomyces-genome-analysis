Comparative Genomics and Physiological Characterization of Streptomyces silvae

This project documents genomic and physiological analyses of S. silvae strains performed during doctoral research.

The study integrates comparative genomics, genome architecture, phylogenomics, and experimental physiology in order to explore genomic diversity and ecological adaptability within the S. silvae clade.

Project highlights. The analyzed strains form a coherent S. silvae group with high genomic similarity, but also show meaningful intraspecific variation in genome structure, biosynthetic potential, and physiological traits.

Project Sections

  • Comparative Genomics – ANI, Mauve alignment, and BGC comparison across the S. silvae clade
  • Genome Architecture – circular genome maps and functional annotation patterns
  • Phylogenomics – genome-based phylogenetic reconstruction using GBDP distances
  • Morphological and Physiological Characterization of Streptomyces silvae TMS4I1 – morphology, stress tolerance, and physiological profiling

Project Overview

The study investigates genomic diversity and ecological adaptability within the S. silvae clade using comparative genomics, genome architecture analysis, phylogenomics, and physiological characterization. Together, these approaches provide a broad view of species coherence, intraspecific diversification, and functional potential.

Genomic perspective

Comparative analysis includes ANI-based similarity assessment, genome-wide synteny analysis, circular genome maps, and biosynthetic gene cluster profiling.

Experimental perspective

Physiological and morphological analyses reveal medium-dependent colony morphology, filamentous microstructure, and broad tolerance towards different environmental stress conditions.
