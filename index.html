Macromorphology of selected S. silvae strains. Colony variation across media reflects phenotypic diversity within the clade.

This website presents doctoral research on S. silvae, integrating DNA preparation, genome assembly, comparative genomics, phylogenomics, genome architecture analysis, biosynthetic gene cluster mining, and physiology.

The genomes of strains TMS4I1 and TMS12I2 were assembled from soil-derived isolates prepared in the laboratory. These two strains were studied experimentally together with the type strain S. silvae For3. Additional genomes included in the comparative analyses were obtained from public databases.

About Me & Expertise

My work focuses on microbial genomics, comparative genome analysis, phylogenomics, genome architecture, and the interpretation of laboratory-derived phenotypes in a genomic context. This project integrates DNA preparation, genome assembly, comparative analysis, representative genome visualization, biosynthetic gene cluster profiling, and experimental physiology within the S. silvae clade.

In this doctoral project, I worked with soil-derived S. silvae isolates in the laboratory, prepared DNA, assembled genomes, and combined these data with additional publicly available genomes for comparative analysis.

The project links genome assembly and comparative bioinformatics with experimental characterization, including morphology and physiological testing. It also includes genome architecture analysis and biosynthetic gene cluster profiling of four representative strains.

Research Areas

The project is organized into four connected analytical sections, each representing a distinct level of interpretation within the S. silvae study.

Comparative Genomics

  • ANI-based strain comparison and genomic relatedness
  • Synteny and alignment-based structural comparison
  • Broad strain-level comparison across the clade
  • Interpretation of conservation and divergence patterns

Linked page: comparative_genomics.html

Phylogenomics

  • Genome-based phylogenetic placement within the clade
  • Reference-based evolutionary context for analyzed strains
  • Taxonomic interpretation supported by genome-scale analysis
  • Framework for connecting comparative results and biology

Linked page: phylogenomics.html

Genome Architecture & BGCs

  • Detailed visualization of four representative genomes
  • Circular genome maps and structural organization
  • BGC localization and comparative biosynthetic context
  • Focused analysis beyond broad strain-level comparison

Physiology

  • Macromorphology and laboratory phenotype assessment
  • Stress tolerance under different environmental conditions
  • Experimental comparison of selected strains
  • Phenotypic layer complementing genomic interpretation
