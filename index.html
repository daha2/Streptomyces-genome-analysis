Project Highlights

The project does not only document methods. It demonstrates how genomic data, evolutionary context, and physiological assays can be integrated to characterize diversity within the S. silvae clade.

Species-level coherence

The analyzed strains form a coherent S. silvae group with strong genomic relatedness, while still showing informative intraspecific variation.

Comparative genome context

ANI, synteny analysis, and genome architecture reveal both conservation and structural divergence across the strain set.

Biosynthetic potential

Biosynthetic gene cluster analysis highlights secondary metabolic capacity and supports interpretation of functional potential.

Genome–phenotype connection

Physiological assays complement the genomic analyses and help relate genetic features to ecological adaptability and stress response.

About Me & Expertise

I work at the interface of microbial systematics, comparative genomics, natural product-oriented genome mining, and experimental physiology.

As part of my doctoral research, I analyzed genomes of marine-derived Streptomyces isolates in order to resolve taxonomic placement, examine genome structure, assess biosynthetic potential, and connect genomic properties with physiological traits.

My work combines sequencing-based analysis with biological interpretation. I value reproducible workflows, clean data presentation, and results that go beyond software output.
