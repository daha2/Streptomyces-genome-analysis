Physiological Characterization of Streptomyces silvae TMS4I1

This page summarizes the physiological characterization and stress tolerance of Streptomyces silvae TMS4I1 based on experimental analyses performed during the dissertation project.

Physiological Characterization and Stress Tolerance

Figure: Physiological characterization and stress tolerance of S. silvae TMS4I1. Unless otherwise indicated, all assays were performed in basal medium 5339 and cultures were incubated for 14 days at 28°C.

(A) Salt tolerance: growth at increasing NaCl concentrations (0, 2.5, 5, 7.5, and 10%);
(B) Hemolysis: β-hemolytic activity on Columbia blood agar, indicated by a clear halo around colonies;
(C) Lysozyme resistance: growth at increasing concentrations of lysozyme (0, 10, 25, 50, 75, and 100 µg/ml);
(D) Temperature optimum: colony growth on MYM agar incubated at constant temperatures ranging from 7 to 37°C;
(E) pH tolerance: growth at pH values from 2 to 10, with incubation on a shaker at 90 rpm.

Interpretation

The physiological profile of Streptomyces silvae TMS4I1 indicates a broad tolerance towards different environmental conditions. The strain shows growth across a wide range of salt concentrations, pH conditions, and temperatures, and also displays resistance to lysozyme as well as pronounced β-hemolytic activity. Together, these observations support the view that strain TMS4I1 is physiologically adaptable and well suited to variable environmental conditions.
