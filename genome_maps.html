Genome Architecture of Streptomyces silvae Strains

Circular genome maps of four Streptomyces strains analysed in the comparative genomic study.

Strains included

  • Streptomyces silvae #12I2
  • Streptomyces silvae #4I1
  • Streptomyces silvae For3T – type strain
  • Streptomyces sp. M3

Detailed information on strain origin and experimental background is described in the associated doctoral dissertation.

Circular Genome Maps

Figure: Circular genome maps of four genomes within the Streptomyces silvae clade. Genome coordinates are shown in megabases (Mb). The tracks visualize gene distribution, RNA genes, GC content, and GC skew along the chromosome.

Genome Map Tracks

Tracks from outer to inner rings:
  1. Genome coordinates (Mb)
  2. Coding sequences on the forward strand
  3. Coding sequences on the reverse strand
  4. RNA genes
  5. GC content
  6. GC skew

Methods

  • Genome scaffolding: RagTag
  • Reference genome: Streptomyces sp. M3
  • Genome annotation: RASTtk
  • Secondary metabolite prediction: antiSMASH
  • Genome visualization: Circos

Interpretation

The genome maps illustrate the conserved genomic architecture within the Streptomyces silvae clade. While the overall structure appears similar across strains, variations in gene distribution and GC content patterns can be observed along the chromosome, reflecting differences in gene content and potential metabolic capabilities.

Reference
