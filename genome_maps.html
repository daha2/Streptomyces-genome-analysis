Genome Architecture of Streptomyces silvae Strains

This page presents circular genome maps of four strains within the Streptomyces silvae clade. The figures summarize genome architecture, contig organization, distribution of coding sequences, RNA genes, antibiotic resistance determinants, GC skew, GC content, and selected assembly and annotation parameters.

Together, these visualizations provide a genome-wide comparison of structural organization and quantitative genome features across the analyzed strains.

Key findings. The analyzed genomes show highly similar genome sizes (approximately 8.1–8.3 Mb) and nearly identical GC contents (~71.3–71.43%), supporting their assignment to the same species. At the same time, differences in contig structure, assembly continuity, and tRNA/rRNA gene copy numbers reveal meaningful intraspecific variation. In particular, strain #4I1 stands out by showing increased gene numbers in several metabolic categories, whereas #12I2, For3T, and M3 are more similar to one another.

Dominant categories

The largest gene fractions are associated with amino acid metabolism and carbohydrate metabolism, indicating that these pathways form the major functional backbone of the analyzed genomes.

Stable low-abundance category

Motility and chemotaxis represent the smallest functional category and show only minimal variation across strains, consistent with the primarily sessile lifestyle of soil-dwelling streptomycetes.

Interpretation. The circular functional profiles show that the four strains are broadly similar in their overall functional gene repertoire, supporting their close relatedness. At the same time, the most pronounced quantitative differences occur in amino acid metabolism, carbohydrate metabolism, lipid and fatty acid metabolism, respiration, and cell wall-associated functions. In particular, strain #4I1 displays elevated gene numbers in several of these categories, in some cases by up to about 22% relative to the other genomes. This suggests strain-specific metabolic adaptation, whereas #12I2, For3T, and M3 remain more similar to one another.

Interpretation of Genome Statistics and Assembly Quality

Comparative genome analysis revealed highly similar genome sizes and GC contents across all four strains, consistent with their classification within the species Streptomyces silvae. Despite this overall similarity, the assemblies differ in contiguity and annotation metrics. Strain M3 shows the highest assembly continuity, whereas the remaining strains are more fragmented. In addition, variation in tRNA and rRNA gene copy numbers indicates functional differences that may reflect strain-specific adaptation.
