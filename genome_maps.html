Circular Genome Maps

Figure: Circular genome maps of four genomes within the Streptomyces silvae clade. From top to bottom: Streptomyces silvae #12I2, Streptomyces silvae #4I1, Streptomyces silvae For3T, and Streptomyces sp. M3. Genome coordinates are shown in megabases (Mb). From the outside to the inside, the tracks represent genome coordinates, coding sequences on the forward strand, coding sequences on the reverse strand, RNA genes, GC content, and GC skew. Protein-coding genes are coloured according to SEED subsystem categories, whereas RNA features are marked separately by RNA type using distinct colours in the annotation track. Positive and negative deviations in GC content and GC skew are shown as peaks, thereby visualising compositional variation and overall structural conservation across the chromosome within the Streptomyces silvae clade.

Methods

  • Genome scaffolding: RagTag
  • Reference genome: Streptomyces sp. M3
  • Genome annotation: RASTtk
  • Secondary metabolite prediction: antiSMASH
  • Genome visualization: Circos

Interpretation

The genome maps illustrate the conserved genomic architecture within the Streptomyces silvae clade. While the overall structure appears similar across strains, variations in gene distribution and GC content patterns can be observed along the chromosome, reflecting differences in gene content and potential metabolic capabilities.

Reference
