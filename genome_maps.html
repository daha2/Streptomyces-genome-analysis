Genome Architecture of Streptomyces silvae Strains

Circular genome maps of four Streptomyces strains analysed in the comparative genomic study.

Strains included

  • Streptomyces silvae #12I2
  • Streptomyces silvae #4I1
  • Streptomyces silvae For3T – type strain
  • Streptomyces sp. M3

Detailed information on strain origin and experimental background is described in the associated doctoral dissertation.

Circular Genome Maps

Figure: Genome maps of the four Streptomyces silvae strains #12I2, #4I1, For3 and M3. The concentric circles arranged from the outside to the inside show, for each genome, the contig lengths in megabase pairs (Mbp) in the first ring, followed by a strain-specific colour coding of the contigs in the second ring. Contigs with a length of < 149 kbp were merged and are shown in white.

The third and fourth rings display the positions of protein-coding genes on the forward and reverse strands, coloured according to their functional categories. The fifth ring marks the localisation of tRNAs and rRNAs, followed by the sixth ring showing antibiotic resistance genes.

The innermost three rings represent positive GC skew, negative GC skew, and GC content.

Methods

  • Genome scaffolding: RagTag
  • Reference genome: Streptomyces sp. M3
  • Genome annotation: RASTtk
  • Genome visualization: Circos

Interpretation

The genome maps illustrate the conserved genomic architecture within the Streptomyces silvae clade. While the overall structure appears similar across strains, variations in gene distribution and compositional patterns can be observed along the chromosome, reflecting differences in gene content and potential metabolic capabilities.

Reference
