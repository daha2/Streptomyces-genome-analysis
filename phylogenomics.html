Phylogenomics of Streptomyces silvae

This page summarizes the genome-based phylogenetic reconstruction of the Streptomyces silvae clade. Phylogenomics was performed using the TYGS platform and Genome BLAST Distance Phylogeny (GBDP).

The resulting tree provides a genome-scale view of evolutionary relationships within the analyzed strains and supports species-level assignment as well as intraspecific diversification.

Key findings. All analyzed strains cluster within a coherent Streptomyces silvae lineage. At the same time, the phylogenomic reconstruction reveals clear internal branching patterns, indicating evolutionary diversification within the species. Closely related genome pairs such as Streptomyces sp. LamerLS–316 and SID4921 group tightly together, while other strains occupy more distinct positions within the clade.

Tree Reconstruction

Phylogenetic reconstruction was based on GBDP distances calculated from complete genome sequences. Tree inference was performed using FastME, and branch support values were calculated using 100 pseudo-bootstrap replicates.

Method

Genome BLAST Distance Phylogeny (GBDP) as implemented in the TYGS platform.

Tree inference

FastME reconstruction with branch lengths scaled according to the GBDP distance formula d5 and support values from 100 replicates.

Interpretation

The phylogenomic tree supports the assignment of all analyzed genomes to the species Streptomyces silvae. At the same time, the branching structure reveals internal phylogenomic differentiation within the clade. This indicates that the species is genomically coherent but not uniform.

The close grouping of certain strains, in particular Streptomyces sp. LamerLS–316 and SID4921, is consistent with their extremely high genome similarity in ANI analyses. Other strains show more distinct phylogenomic positions, reflecting evolutionary diversification within the species.
